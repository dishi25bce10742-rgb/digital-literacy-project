# Case Study: Cyberbullying

## What is Cyberbullying?
Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms — such as social media, 
messaging apps, or online forums — to harass, threaten, or humiliate 
another person repeatedly over time.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The bully identifies a target, often someone they know personally 
   or someone who is active on social media.
2. They begin sending hurtful, mocking, or threatening messages 
   privately or publicly.
3. Others may join in, spreading screenshots or making fun of the 
   victim in group chats (this is called a "pile-on").
4. The bully may create fake accounts to continue harassment even 
   after being blocked.
5. The victim's personal information may be shared publicly 
   (known as "doxxing") to cause further harm.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
Cyberbullying most commonly targets teenagers and young adults, 
particularly those who are socially isolated, belong to minority 
groups, or have recently experienced a public embarrassment online. 
College students are increasingly affected due to heavy social 
media usage.

## Consequences
- Severe anxiety, depression, and loss of self-confidence in victims
- Academic performance drops due to emotional distress
- In extreme cases, cyberbullying has led to self-harm or suicide
- Legal consequences for the bully under India's IT Act, 2000 
  (Section 66A) and IPC sections related to harassment
